How to Take Care of Diagnostic Instruments?

Stethoscopes

Stethoscopes can be damaged by extreme temperatures and with time face problems with tubing. Therefore, you will need to leave that scope in extreme heat or cold over extended periods of time. Exposing them to oils or solvents might cause damage to tubes and chest pieces. Exposure to sunlight can cause considerable damage to the tubes, retain rings diaphragms on the stethoscope. Therefore, they need to be kept at a place where it will be exposed to direct sunlight over long periods of time. When your stethoscopes become dirty, you will need to wipe it down mild soapy water. They need to be kept dry after cleaning., before being wiped down with a 70% isopropyl alcohol solution for low level disinfection. The ear tips on the stethoscope need to be removed by unscrewing them from binaurals. This will enable thorough cleaning to be done.

Blood Pressure Monitors

As blood pressure monitor works automatically, it will need to be re-calibrated at least every two years to acquire the best results. To re-calibrate your automatic home monitor, send it back to the manufacturer. Companies usually perform recalibration for a fee.

Oximeters

A pulse oximeter requires routine maintenance and tests to maintain their accuracy. Check it to ensure that the device is clean and decontaminated. Therefore, check whether there is any type of physical damage to the display, case, mounts or components. The switches and controls need to be operated and aligned correctly. Find out if the control numbers, warnings and labels are present. If they do, find out whether they are legible or not. Check the inlets, hoses and power cord and find out whether they are functioning properly or not. Ensure that both filters and vents are clean.

Thermometers

Clean your thermometer after exposure too dirty, dusky, humid and smoky conditions. Regular cleaning is essential in every six months. Utilize a soft cloth or cotton swab with media or medical alcohol. But don’t use soap or chemicals. Wipe the lens carefully first and then body of the thermometer. Let the lens dry fully before using the thermometer. But don’t immerse any part of the thermometer. Store thermometer between 40°F and 149°F (4-65°C). Store the thermometer in a place which is not exposed to extreme temperature.

Glucometers

As in case of other medical devices, glucometers need regular maintenance to function properly. Since your treatment depends of readings of a glucose machine, proper maintenance of a glucometer is must to ensure accuracy in blood glucose values. Here are some steps that you can take to make sure that proper care of glucometer:

Daily cleaning- A number of glucometers come with lens that are streaked with blood and oil from dust and fingers. Avoid using alcohol since it can damage the glucometer. Rather use plain water or special cleaner which is suitable for cleaning sensitive equipment. Use a soft cloth to gently to wipe and clean the affected area.

Once cleaning is done, check all parts to find whether they are neat. Also verify whether there is any physical damage to the case, display, mounts, components or cart. The switches and controls need to be operable and perfectly aligned. Find out whether the control numbers, warnings and labels are present. At the next step check the hoses and inlets to find out if the power cord and charger are functioning properly or not. Therefore, learn to use the glucometer and make it a habit to change its battery from time to time. You can also keep a spare set of batteries in case you want a last minute testing to be done.

Utilize the solution like a blood sample and compare its reading with the desired range on the control packaging. If you find that the control is out of range, shake the solution in a better manner, clean the lens, or replace your batteries.

Utilize the Check Strip or Key- Once you open a new bottle or test strip, don’t forget to use the check strip or key to set your glucometer’s code.
